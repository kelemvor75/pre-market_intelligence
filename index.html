Investment Thesis

One of Only Two U.S. Companies Capable of Large-Scale Advanced Packaging. Amkor is the world’s #2 outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) provider, and one of only two U.S.-headquartered companies (alongside ASE) capable of delivering advanced packaging solutions at hyperscale volume. Every AI chip — from NVIDIA’s GB200 to AMD’s MI400 — requires advanced packaging (2.5D/3D, fan-out, CoWoS-like) to stack HBM and integrate chiplets. Amkor’s management guides the AI advanced packaging portfolio to triple in 2026, with two new CPU HDFO programs entering high-volume production in H2.

Four Consecutive Earnings Beats and Record Q1 Revenue. Q1 2026 revenue of $1.685B grew 27% YoY with gross margins expanding to 14.2% (above the 13.5% guidance midpoint). EPS of $0.33 beat the $0.23 consensus by 43%. Q2 is guided to $1.80–$1.90B with margins of 14.5–15.5%. The Investor Day on May 21 set a $11B+ revenue target by 2030 (from $6.7B in 2025), underpinned by the Arizona facility ramp and NVIDIA partnership.

The Arizona Facility Is the CHIPS Act Packaging Play. Amkor is building a $2B+ advanced packaging facility in Peoria, Arizona — the first large-scale advanced packaging fab in the United States. It will serve as the domestic packaging partner for chips made at TSMC Arizona and Intel’s foundry. Capex guidance of $2.5–$3.0B for 2026 reflects the aggressive buildout. This positions Amkor as essential infrastructure for the reshoring of the U.S. semiconductor supply chain.

2  |  BUSINESS MODEL & COMPETITIVE POSITION
Revenue Architecture

Advanced Packaging (~50%, fastest growing): 2.5D/3D integration, fan-out wafer-level packaging (FOWLP), high-density fan-out (HDFO), and flip chip. This is where the AI growth lives — every AI accelerator, HBM stack, and chiplet design requires advanced packaging. Management says this portfolio will triple in 2026.

Mainstream Packaging (~35%): Wire bonding, leadframe, and traditional IC packaging for automotive, IoT, and consumer electronics. Stable but lower-margin. Provides revenue base during semiconductor downturns.

Test Services (~15%): Wafer-level and final test for packaged devices. Growing with complexity as advanced packages require more sophisticated test regimes.

Amkor’s competitive position is defined by a single structural trend: the world needs more advanced packaging capacity than exists. TSMC’s CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) is sold out through 2027. Every hyperscaler, every AI chip designer, and every CPU company is scrambling for packaging capacity. Amkor and ASE are the only two OSATs with the technology and scale to absorb overflow from TSMC’s in-house packaging. The Arizona facility makes Amkor the domestic packaging option for chips fabbed at TSMC Arizona and Intel’s Ohio fabs — a geopolitically essential role.

The business model risk is margin compression. Amkor’s gross margins (14%) are thin compared to equipment companies (Lam 47%, AMAT 47%) or fabless designers (NVDA 73%, AMD 53%). The OSAT model is inherently capital-intensive and low-margin — Amkor spends $2.5–$3.0B in annual capex to generate ~$7.5B in revenue at 14% gross margin. The bull case requires margins expanding to 18–20% as advanced packaging mix increases and Arizona utilization ramps. Management’s Investor Day target of 30% incremental flow-through implies this margin expansion is achievable, but it hasn’t been proven yet.

9  |  WHAT WOULD CHANGE OUR MIND

We would downgrade from BUY if:

Gross margins fail to reach 16% by Q4 2026. Would indicate the advanced packaging mix shift isn’t driving margin expansion as management promises.

CEO Kim’s insider selling continues at current pace. Would signal the founding family is exiting, not repositioning.

Arizona facility ramp delayed beyond H2 2026. Would undermine the domestic packaging thesis and CHIPS Act narrative.

TSMC expands CoWoS capacity enough to eliminate OSAT overflow. Would remove the structural supply shortage that underpins Amkor’s pricing power.

10  |  PRICE TARGET & CONCLUSION
Price Target: $85  |  Rating: BUY

Our $85 target represents 29% upside from $66.06, derived from 25x FY2027E EPS of $3.20 plus a premium for the Arizona facility optionality and the structural advanced packaging shortage.

Amkor is the most polarizing stock in our coverage. The bull case is powerful: one of only two companies in the world capable of large-scale advanced packaging, AI packaging tripling in 2026, two new CPU programs launching in H2, the Arizona facility making Amkor essential to the U.S. semiconductor supply chain, and a clear path from $6.7B to $11B+ revenue by 2030. Four consecutive earnings beats and yesterday’s Investor Day reinforce the trajectory.

The bear case is equally compelling: 14% gross margins in a capital-intensive business, $487.5M equity dilution, $485M in insider selling by the founding family with zero purchases, 12% short interest (highest in our coverage), and a stock trading at consensus with 50% of analysts rating it Hold. The conviction score of 68/100 reflects this genuine tension — we believe the growth thesis will win over the margin concerns as the advanced packaging mix shift materializes in H2, but we acknowledge the insider selling pattern is a red flag that demands monitoring.
