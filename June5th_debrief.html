Bottom line: this is not the "buy-the-semi-dip" setup that paid off all through 2024–25. Those dips bottomed in rate-cutting / capitulation regimes. Friday is a shock from record highs into a rate-hike regime — and history says that combination produces muted, coin-flip forward returns with a retest near the 2-week mark and the deepest air-pocket around 2 months.
  • 1 week: mild oversold-bounce tendency (tightening-regime QQQ median +2.3%), but fully gated by May CPI on Jun 10.
  • 2 weeks: the retest window — across every conditioning, day-10 is the weakest, sub-50% positive, and it contains the FOMC on Jun 16–17.
  • 1 month: roughly flat / range-bound, below-normal (≈55% positive vs ~64% unconditional).
  • 2–3 months: the genuine fork. In an active-hiking regime (2018, 2022) QQQ bottomed ≈−4.7% near 2 months with only 29% positive. A clean recovery by 3 months is the base case only if the rate / inflation / oil shock abates.
  • The dominant variable is rates, not Broadcom. The AI-thesis scare tends to mean-revert; the macro is what decides the medium-term path.

Trigger 1 — AVGO. Broadcom beat Q2 (rev $22.19B vs $22.13B, EPS $2.44 vs $2.39) but guided Q3 AI chip sales to ~$16B vs $17.2B expected and declined to raise its FY26 AI forecast. Classic sell-the-news: AVGO −14% Thu, −7.9% Fri. The tape read it as a tell on the AI-capex cycle and dumped the whole chain (NVDA −6.2%, MRVL −8%, TXN −6.7%, MU/AMD −6%+).

Trigger 2 — the jobs report. May payrolls printed +172k vs +80–85k expected, unemployment steady at 4.3%. Because inflation is already hot (Apr CPI +0.6% m/m, 3.8% y/y; core 2.8%) and there's an active Iran-war oil shock (Brent >$84), a strong labor read pushed the market to almost fully price a 2026 rate hike. 10y → 4.54% (+6bp), 2y → 4.16% (+10bp), 30y > 5%. Yields up = high-multiple tech down.

The tell that matters most: VIX entered Friday near 15–16.
The selloff began from complacency at record highs (S&P closed at an all-time high on Jun 2), not from an already-panicked tape. Every historical SOXX −10% day bottomed markets — but those came after extended declines with VIX at 45–82 (Oct-2002, Mar-2020, Apr-2025). A −10% semi day from a 15-VIX record high is structurally the front end of a vol expansion, not the back end. That single fact argues against treating Friday as a capitulation low.

02 · Why the regime rewrites the answer the core insight

Same-sized selloff, opposite outcomes — depending entirely on what the Fed was doing.

The naïve backtest is a trap. Filter history to SOXX one-day drops of −10% and you get only three priors — Oct-2002, Mar-2020, Apr-2025 — and forward returns look spectacular (SMH median +43% at 3 months, 100% positive). But all three were capitulation bottoms inside Fed-easing or policy-rescue regimes. The easing caused the V. Friday has the opposite macro. To get an honest read, you have to condition on the rate regime.

03 · The shock-from-highs signature path shape

Independent of rates, there's a second conditioner that fits Friday: the selloff hit from within 8% of a 1-year high (Friday's QQQ drawdown was just -5.5%). These "shocks from strength" share a distinctive shape — a quick stabilization, then a second leg / retest around day 10 (only ~25% positive), then recovery by 2–3 months.

08 · What flips the thesis the two-sided watch list

↑ Bull triggers (→ Jan-2026 round-trip)
  • Soft May CPI (≤0.2% core) — rate-hike pricing unwinds
  • Brent rolls back under ~$75 on Iran de-escalation
  • FOMC holds with a balanced/dovish-leaning tone
  • MU (6/24) delivers a strong HBM/AI guide — capex thesis intact
  • VIX fails to hold above ~22 and rolls over
↓ Bear triggers (→ 2018/2022 derating)
  • Hot CPI/PCE — a hike gets pulled forward or delivered
  • 10y breaks decisively above ~4.6–4.7%; 2y holds >4.2%
  • Warsh frames inflation as the sole priority
  • Oil spikes on Hormuz disruption
  • A second AI-capex tell (hyperscaler capex cut / weak NVDA read) confirms the peak fear

Net judgment: respect the short-term bounce but don't mistake it for the all-clear. The historical edge in this regime says fade-prone and range-bound into a ~2-month trough, with the rate path as the swing factor and a genuine recovery contingent on the inflation/oil shock abating. For a concentrated, high-beta AI-infra book, that argues for patience on adds (let the day-10 retest and FOMC clear), keeping dry powder for the 2-month air-pocket, and treating MU (6/24) as the key thesis-confirmation read — rather than reflexively buying the first green candle as the last two years trained.

Methodology & caveats. Forward returns are computed on Tiingo split/dividend-adjusted daily closes, 2001–2026. "Selloff events" are declustered (≥10 trading days apart, worst day retained) one-day drops in QQQ (≤−4%) or SOXX (≤−8% / −10%). Regime tags use FRED fed-funds (6-month change), 10y yield, and the trailing-252-day QQQ drawdown. "Median / %pos" = median forward total return and share of episodes positive, excluding the Jun-5 anchor itself. SOXX (continuous iShares since 2001) is used for the long semi history; SMH forward returns are clean post-2012. Sample sizes are small — the tightening-bias set is n=11 and the active-hiking set is n=7, so confidence intervals are wide and these are base rates, not forecasts. Overlapping forward windows are autocorrelated, which understates true standard errors. Analogs are imperfect by construction; no two regimes are identical, and the Iran oil shock + a brand-new Fed chair are genuinely novel variables with no clean historical match.
